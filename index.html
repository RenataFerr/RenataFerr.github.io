I am a theoretical physicist and postdoctoral researcher at the Institute for Quantum Gravity of the Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nürnberg in Germany.

About Me

My work spans areas from the asymptotic safety scenario to the canonical approach to quantum gravity. My aim is to understand the connection between geometry, gravity, and physical reference systems, and to seek observable imprints of quantum gravity.

From 2019 to 2023 I did my doctoral studies at the University of Mainz in Germany. In December 2023 I obtained my PhD (summa cum laude), for which I was awarded the Wilhelm and Else Heraeus dissertation price for the year 2023/2024.

Since November 2023 I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Institute for Quantum Gravity at the Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nürnberg in Germany.

I am very active in promoting women and diversity in science. During my doctoral studies I was a Ada Lovelace fellow, and have been a mentor in the Cybermentor program, which provides support for girls in STEM subjects. At the FAU Erlangen I am part of the Gender and Diversity group, and am coorganizing the DIPHER 2024 conference (Diversity in physics for the diversity of physics - Erlangen).
